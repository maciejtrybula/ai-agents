# Modern SEO & AI-First Optimization Guidelines (2026)

## 1. The Paradigm Shift: From SEO to GEO
In 2026, search is driven by Large Language Models (LLMs). Optimization must target both traditional crawlers (Googlebot) and AI aggregators (Gemini, SearchGPT, Perplexity).

### Core Pillars of GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)
* **Citation-Centric Content:** AI engines prioritize sources they can cite. Use clear, factual claims backed by unique data.
* **Brand Entity Authority:** Ensure your brand is recognized as an "entity" in the Knowledge Graph. Use consistent naming across the web.
* **Direct Answer Optimization:** Structure content to answer the "Who, What, Why" in the first 200 characters of a section.

## 2. E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness)
* **Experience:** AI cannot "experience" things. Content that proves first-hand use, original photos, or personal case studies is prioritized.
* **Expertise:** Use "Author Clusters." Link articles to deeply researched bios and LinkedIn profiles.

## 3. Technical SEO for AI & Humans
* **Semantic HTML5:** Use tags (`<article>`, `<aside>`, `<section>`) to help LLMs parse the hierarchy of information.
* **JSON-LD Structured Data:** This is the "API for Search." Use Schema.org for:
    * `FAQPage` (Direct answers)
    * `TechArticle` (Technical documentation)
    * `ProfilePage` (Author authority)
* **Core Web Vitals (INP focus):** *Interaction to Next Paint* is the primary metric for user satisfaction.

## 4. NLP & Semantic Search
* **Latent Semantic Indexing (LSI):** Use related terms, not just keywords. If the topic is "TypeScript," the content must include "Interface," "Generics," and "Transpilation."
* **Conversational Queries:** Optimize for "How do I..." or "What is the best way to..." rather than "Best TypeScript tips."

## 5. Future-Proofing
* **RAG-Friendly Formatting:** Use clear headings and bullet points. LLMs using Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) find it easier to "chunk" and retrieve this data.
* **Zero-Click Optimization:** Provide value in the snippet. If the user gets the answer on the SERP, the engine views your site as the definitive source.
