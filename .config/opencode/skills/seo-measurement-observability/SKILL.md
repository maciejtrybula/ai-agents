# SEO Measurement and Observability

## Goals

- Track whether SEO changes improve discoverability, traffic quality, and conversions.
- Detect crawl, indexation, rendering, and template regressions early.
- Connect SEO work to business outcomes and experimentation.

## Measurement areas

- Search performance: impressions, clicks, CTR, average position, landing-page segmentation.
- Technical health: crawl status, sitemap coverage, indexation changes, structured-data errors, Core Web Vitals.
- Content performance: intent coverage, engagement, assisted conversions, freshness decay.
- Future-facing signals: AI brand mentions, citation frequency, answer-engine visibility, zero-click behavior.

## Workflow

1. Define KPIs and reporting granularity.
2. Map source systems such as analytics, search console, crawl tools, logs, and performance monitoring.
3. Build dashboards for executive, product, and operational views.
4. Add alerting for sudden drops in crawling, indexation, CTR, performance, or template integrity.
5. Review experiment results and feed learnings back into SEO priorities.

## Output expectations

- Recommend leading and lagging indicators.
- Distinguish diagnostics from business KPIs.
- Include alert thresholds and ownership suggestions.
- Prefer simple, durable reporting over vanity dashboards.
